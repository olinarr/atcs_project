'''J. Joe Ricketts''' (born July 16, 1941) is the founder, former CEO and former chairman of [[TD Ameritrade]], one of the largest online discount brokerages in the world, based in [[Omaha, Nebraska]]. <ref>{{cite web
|url=http://finance.yahoo.com/q/pr?s=AMTD |title=Yahoo Finance: AMTD
|publisher=Yahoo Finance
}}</ref> In addition to serving as a Director of TD Ameritrade, Ricketts owns and operates several early stage companies, including DNAinfo.com, [[American Film Company (2008)|The American Film Company]], High Plains Bison, and The Lodge at Jackson Fork Ranch.  . 

==Personal life==. 
Ricketts was born and raised in [[Nebraska City, Nebraska]] and obtained a bachelor's degree from [[Creighton University]] in Omaha, Nebraska in 1968. <ref>[http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-54622111.html Highbeam], May 1999</ref> Ricketts is married with four children.  In 2009, Ricketts ranked #371, according to [[Forbes Magazine]], of the 400 wealthiest Americans, with a net worth of $1 billion.  He currently resides in [[Little Jackson Hole, Wyoming]]. <ref>[http://www.forbes.com/lists/2009/54/rich-list-09_J-Joseph-Ricketts_4H9T.html Forbes], Sept 2009</ref> . 

==TD Ameritrade==. 
In 1975, Ricketts and a few partners formed [[First Omaha Securities]], a retail securities brokerage firm that through mergers and acquisitions grew into [[TD Ameritrade]]. <ref>[http://www.forbes.com/global/1999/0705/0213104a.html Forbes], July 1999</ref>  . 

Ricketts announced that he would retire from the [[TD Ameritrade]] board to concentrate on entrepreneurship and philanthropy, effective October 20, 2011. <ref>[http://www.reuters.com/article/2011/09/16/idUS125921+16-Sep-2011+PRN20110916 Reuters], Sept 2011</ref> . 

==Business ventures==. 
In 2004, Ricketts founded High Plains Bison, a retailer of natural bison meat. In addition to online and offline sales channels, High Plains Bison is the official bison vendor at Chicago’s [[Wrigley Field]]. <ref>[http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2010-05-06/news/ct-play-0506-vettel-ballpark-wrigley-20100506_1_hot-dog-captain-morgan-club-wrigley ChicagoTribune.com], May 2010</ref> Some of the bison are raised on a Wyoming ranch owned by Ricketts. A four-bedroom lodge named The Lodge at Jackson Fort Ranch is located on the same property. <ref>[http://www.yellowstonegeotourism.org/content/lodge-at-jackson-fork-ranch/yelEE42F008083CBA8B2 YellowstoneGeoTourism.org]</ref><ref>[http://www.time.com/time/travel/article/0,31542,1904610,00.html Time.com], June 2009</ref> . 

In 2008, Ricketts founded [[American Film Company (2008)|The American Film Company]], which produces feature films about true stories from American history. <ref>[http://www.nytimes.com/2011/04/10/movies/td-ameritrades-founder-produces-historical-film.html New York Times], April 2011</ref> ''[[The Conspirator]]'' is a 2010 [[historical drama film]] directed by [[Robert Redford]]. It served as the debut film of The American Film Company. <ref>[http://blogs.wsj.com/speakeasy/2010/09/12/toronto-film-festival-robert-redford-on-the-conspirator Wall Street Journal, Speakeasy], Sept 2010</ref><ref>[http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2010/09/15/redfords-film-is-picked-up-in-toronto/ New York Times ArtsBeat], Sept 2010</ref> . 

In 2009, Ricketts founded DNAinfo.com, a digital news service that currently covers neighborhood news in New York City. <ref>[http://www.crainsnewyork.com/article/20101205/SMALLBIZ/312059973 Crains New York], Dec 2010</ref><ref>[http://www.observer.com/2010/media/three-birds-billionaire-and-hyper-local-future-news NY Observer], May 2010</ref> . 

==Chicago Cubs baseball team==. 
In October 2009, the Ricketts family acquired a 95 percent controlling interest in [[Major League Baseball]]’s [[Chicago Cubs]] and [[Wrigley Field]], as well as 25 percent of Comcast Sportsnet Chicago. The Ricketts family represents the eighth ownership group in the 133-year history of the team. While Ricketts is not directly involved in the team’s operations, his son, [[Thomas S. Ricketts|Tom Ricketts]], is Cubs chairman and his three other children ([[Pete Ricketts|Pete]], [[Laura Ricketts|Laura]] and Todd) are on the board of directors. <ref>{{cite web
|url=http://chicago.cubs.mlb.com/news/press_releases/press_release.jsp?ymd=20091030&content_id=7582312&vkey=pr_chc&fext=.jsp&c_id=chc
|title=Ricketts Family Assumes Control of Chicago Cubs
|publisher=cubs.com
|date=2009-10-30}}</ref> In November 2010, the Cubs announced a plan to seek $200 million in state-backed bonds for renovations to Wrigley Field. <ref>[http://chicagobreakingbusiness.com/2010/11/ricketts-no-plan-b-if-state-wont-back-wrigley-bonds.html ChicagoBreakingBusiness.com], November 2010</ref> . 

==Political activities==. 
In 2010, Ricketts led a campaign against special-interest [[Earmark (politics)|earmarks]] and wasteful spending in the federal government. <ref>[http://dyn.politico.com/members/forums/thread.cfm?catid=7&subcatid=41&threadid=4573155 Politico], Sept 2010</ref><ref>[http://www.politico.com/news/stories/1110/45225.html Politico], Nov 2010</ref> Ricketts founded a nonpartisan organization called Taxpayers Against Earmarks and classified every Member of Congress as either a spending “hero” or “hooligan.”<ref>[http://thecaucus.blogs.nytimes.com/2010/09/30/ricketts-joins-the-big-leagues-with-earmarks-crusade/ NYTimes The Caucus], Sept 2010</ref> Ricketts has been credited with convincing Members of Congress to adopt a moratorium on earmarks. <ref>[http://www.politico.com/news/stories/1210/46060.html Politico], Dec 2010</ref><ref>[http://dailycaller.com/2010/11/16/how-conservatives-beat-the-establishment-on-earmarks/ Dailycaller.com], Nov 2010</ref> Taxpayers Against Earmarks changed its name to Ending Spending in 2011. <ref>[http://dailycaller.com/2011/07/27/ending-spendings-launches-debt-limit-ads/ Dailycaller.com], July 2011</ref> . 

Ricketts established and funded The Ending Spending Fund, a political action committee, in 2010. <ref>[http://www.momaha.com/article/20101130/NEWS01/711309938/1101866#joe-ricketts-at-front-in-earmarks-fight Omaha.com], Nov 2010</ref> The Ending Spending Fund spent over $1 million sponsoring independent advertisements in several Congressional races. The goal of the advertising expenditure was to highlight incumbents’ earmark-related policies. <ref>[http://www.momaha.com/article/20101130/NEWS01/711309938/1101866#joe-ricketts-at-front-in-earmarks-fight Omaha.com], Nov 2010</ref><ref>[http://politics.blogs.foxnews.com/2010/10/28/new-tv-ad-ending-spending-fund-targets-reid-over-earmarks Foxnews.com], Oct 2010</ref> The Ending Spending Fund spent the largest amount of its money on the [[United States Senate election in Nevada, 2010]] in an effort to defeat Senate Majority Leader [[Harry Reid]]. Although not required by law, Ricketts willingly disclosed his identity due to his belief in [[Transparency (behavior)|transparency]]. <ref>{{cite web
|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2010/10/21/super-pac-taxpayers-earmarks-concerned-citizens-campaign-finance_n_772214.html
|title=The One-Person Funded Super PAC: How Wealthy Donors Can Skirt Campaign Finance Restrictions
|first=Amanda
|last=Terkel
|publisher=Huffington Post
|date=2010-10-22}}</ref> . 

Ricketts served on the board of trustees of the American Enterprise Institute from 1999-2007. <ref>[http://www.aei.org/article/15922 AEI.org]</ref> .

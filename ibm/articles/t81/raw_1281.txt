'''Lack of [[Physical Education]]''' is the inadequacy of the provision and effectiveness of [[exercise]] and physical activity within modern [[education]]. <ref>{{citation |title=The disappearance of physical education  |author=Shannon W. Weed |year=2005 |url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=SaSDHAAACAAJ}}</ref> . 

More than a third of young people in grades 9-12 do not regularly engage in vigorous physical activity.  Daily participation in high school physical education classes dropped from 42% in 1991 to 28% in 2003. <ref>{{citation |title=Lack of Physical Activity |url=http://www2.state.id.us/phd7/hpps/Promotion/PAN/Lack%20of%20physical%20Activity.htm}}</ref> . 

Nearly 10 million children and adolescents in the United States ages 6â€“19 are considered [[overweight]] because the lack of physical education in schools.  <ref>{{citation |url=http://www.americanheart.org/presenter.jhtml?identifier=3010854 |title=Physical Education in Schools}}</ref>  Children aged 6-11 have also more than doubled in rate of [[obesity]] over the last twenty years. <ref>{{citation |url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=eGNXNAAACAAJ |title=Childhood obesity and the lack of physical education programs |author=Leslie Haub |year=2007}}</ref> . 

==Causes==  
The causes of the increasing lack of physical education include [[budget|budgetary pressure]] which limits the resources provided for this; the increasing attractiveness of rival pastimes such as [[video game]]s; and the increased emphasis upon [[examination|academic results]]. <ref>{{citation |url=http://www.boston.com/news/local/massachusetts/articles/2007/05/30/lack_of_physical_education_weighs_heavily_on_many/ |title=Lack of physical education weighs heavily on many |publisher=The Boston Globe |date=Lisa Wangsness and April Simpson |date=May 30, 2007}}</ref> .

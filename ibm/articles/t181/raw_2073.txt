The '''Universal Declaration on Cultural Diversity''' is a declaration adopted by the General Conference of the [[UNESCO|United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO)]] at its thirty-first session on 2 November 2001<ref name="UNCommiss01">[http://www2.ohchr.org/english/law/diversity.htm Office of the United Nations High Commissioner of Human Rights 'Universal Declaration of Cultural Diversity' webpage]</ref> . 

This Declaration is constituted by 12 Articles; Article 1 titled "Cultural diversity, the common heritage of humanity" states that "As a sorce of exchange, [[innovation]] and creativity, cultural diversity is as necessary for humankind as [[biodiversity]] is for the nature. In this sense, it is the common heritage of humanity and should be recognized and affiemed for the benefit of present and future generations." And Article 12 defines on the role of the UNESCO.  .
